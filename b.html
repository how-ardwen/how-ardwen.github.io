howard wen


The houses pass; glimpsing through
The window I see you.
Transient, but I get so caught up in it all.
What could be, had that been the stop.

These routes blur, burr, contort,
So resplendent and susurrant!

Mine, if I step out
So we establish that existence necessitates dream.
Then the punishment is temporality
And then my buboes of regret pus and bulge -
One for every house.

Do I get off? My map is planar, my body cylindrical
Reduced to geometries, distorted by dilation
I'm nauseous from the thinking.

So I cease. This is a eulogy for our infinite closing doors. For
I do not bathe in yesterday's sun nor glow in yesterday's moon.
I do not swim on yesterday's shore nor dance in yesterday's grass.

Angle East. Footwise.
Like a pawn on the marble, march.
Let it ring on your scarred and knotted bone.
